Every Stage

Illness Does Not Move in a Straight Line

The illness journey has no single shape. It moves forward, backward, sideways, and sometimes seems to stop entirely. SolaceCare is present at every turn.

Stage 1

When the News Is New

For those who have just received a diagnosis.

  • Shock, disbelief, confusion
  • Overwhelming decisions
  • Feeling frozen or numb
"You don't need answers right now. You need someone who will listen and help you breathe."
Stage 2

During Treatment

For those currently in treatment or living with active illness.

  • Physical pain and exhaustion
  • Anxiety and fear
  • Isolation and emotional overload
  • Caregivers carrying silent weight
"We listen when treatment feels endless and strength feels thin."
Stage 3

After Treatment

For those who are done with treatment but not yet healed.

  • Chronic pain or disability
  • Medical debt and job loss
  • Depression and loneliness
  • The world expects you to move on
"Survival is not the same as recovery. We stay when everyone else has moved on."

The SolaceCare Hotline

A Place to Call — No Matter Where You Are

The SolaceCare Hotline is a confidential, peer-led listening line for individuals whose lives have been impacted by serious illness. It is not therapy. It is not medical advice. It is human presence, without an agenda.

Who It's For

  • People newly diagnosed with a serious illness
  • Patients currently in treatment
  • Survivors who are still hurting
  • People living with illness-related chronic pain, disability, or addiction
  • Caregivers carrying the weight of someone else's illness

What Happens When You Call

You speak with a trained listener with lived experience of serious illness
You are not rushed, judged, or told how to feel
You may talk, cry, sit in silence, or simply be present
You are met with patience, respect, and genuine care

Our Story

Why SolaceCare Exists

Victor Kilanko was diagnosed with stage IV cancer, described at the time as terminal. Treatment saved his life, but it permanently altered it. He couldn't walk for months. He was afraid, anxious, and financially devastated. When treatment ended, support largely disappeared. The pain remained.

What he learned is simple and devastating: most care systems are built for treatment, not for the life that follows.

SolaceCare Network was created to fill that gap, offering listening, presence, and dignity at every stage of the journey.

"Sometimes the most healing thing is knowing that someone will stay on the line."

Our Mission

To listen with compassion and without judgment to people affected by serious illness, before, during, and after treatment, offering steadiness, dignity, and human connection when life is disrupted or permanently changed.
